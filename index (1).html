The Project

Supply Chain Optimization Study: Where is the money hiding?

A mid-sized industrial distributor was flying blind — leadership knew inventory was too high and service levels were slipping, but nobody could say where or why. I analyzed 500 SKUs across 20 vendors to find out.

01

The Problem

Leadership couldn't answer basic questions: which SKUs are overstocked? Which vendors are dragging service? Where is working capital locked up? Classic symptoms of data living in silos.

02

The Approach

I pulled procurement, inventory, and vendor performance data, joined them into one view, and built a dashboard to surface cost, stock, and service-level issues at the SKU and vendor level.

03

The Outcome

Identified $2.1M in recoverable working capital and revenue exposure, flagged 5 vendors for strategic review, and delivered a roadmap for ongoing inventory and service optimization.

Key Findings

Five insights that would change the business.

A dashboard is only as useful as the decisions it drives. Here's what the data told me to do.

FINDING 01

Nearly half the inventory is tying up working capital

44.8% of SKUs are overstocked against policy — with $912K in locked inventory. Most of it sits in Packaging and Components, where ordering patterns don't match demand velocity.

→ $912,390 in recoverable working capital
FINDING 02

50 SKUs are at active stockout risk

10% of the catalog has less than 7 days of stock on hand. These SKUs represent $1.25M in annual revenue — and they're one supply disruption away from hurting service levels.

→ $1.25M revenue exposure
FINDING 03

5 vendors hold 22.5% of spend while underperforming

The bottom 5 vendors by on-time delivery average below 75%, yet still hold $2.2M in annual spend. That's a strategic sourcing problem, not a vendor management problem.

→ $2.24M of spend needs repositioning
FINDING 04

Lead times vary wildly — but policy is uniform

Lead times range from 3 to 90 days. Applying a uniform safety stock rule means we're either over-investing in fast-moving items or under-protecting slow-moving ones.

→ SKU-level policy needed, not global
FINDING 05

Two categories drive more than half the spend

Packaging (28.5%) and Components (23.1%) together are over half of all procurement. Any sourcing, negotiation, or inventory work starts here — biggest surface area for impact.

→ 51.6% of spend in two categories
FINDING 06

Data integrity is the real bottleneck

The hardest part wasn't the analysis — it was reconciling procurement, inventory, and finance data that lived in separate systems. Without a single source of truth, none of the above insights were visible.

→ Master data is the foundation

PYTHON · PANDAS

Data cleaning & reconciliation

Before any analysis, procurement, inventory, and vendor data had to be joined, de-duplicated, and validated. Sample snippet below.

df = pd.merge(procurement, inventory, on='SKU', how='outer')
df['Days_On_Hand'] = df['Current_Stock'] / (df['Annual_Demand'] / 365)
df['Stock_Status'] = pd.cut(df['Days_On_Hand'],
    bins=[0, 7, 30, 90, float('inf')],
    labels=['Stockout Risk', 'Healthy', 'Overstocked', 'Severely Overstocked']
)
SQL · KPI LOGIC

Vendor performance scoring

Aggregated vendor-level metrics across 500 SKUs to identify both cost and service risks in one view.

SELECT
  vendor,
  COUNT(sku) AS sku_count,
  SUM(annual_spend) AS total_spend,
  AVG(on_time_delivery_rate) AS avg_otd,
  AVG(lead_time_days) AS avg_lead_time
FROM supply_chain
GROUP BY vendor
ORDER BY avg_otd ASC;
ANALYSIS

Why master data is the real bottleneck

The hardest part of this project wasn't the model — it was reconciling three systems that described the same SKU differently. Key issues I had to resolve:

  • SKU codes with inconsistent casing and trailing spaces
  • Vendor names that varied between systems (e.g., "Acme Corp." vs. "Acme Corporation")
  • Currency and unit conversion across regions
  • Missing lead-time data for 8% of SKUs — filled via vendor-level averages
